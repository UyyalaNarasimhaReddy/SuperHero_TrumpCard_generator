Captain America

Captain America, alias Steve Rogers, is a Marvel Comics superhero created in 1941. Enhanced during World War II, he wields a vibranium shield and embodies American ideals, serving as a symbol of patriotism and justice in comics, films, and the Marvel Cinematic Universe.

Detailed Info




The alter ego of Captain America is Steve Rogers, a frail young man enhanced to the peak of human physicality by an experimental serum in order to aid the United States government's efforts during World War II. He wears a costume that bears the American flag motif and is armed with a nearly indestructible shield that he throws as a projectile. Captain America is often referred to as the "Sentinel of Liberty" and the "Star-Spangled Avenger. Steve Rogers began his career as a superhero during the Golden Age of Comic Books, fighting against the Axis powers and their supervillain counterparts. He quickly became a symbol of American patriotism and resilience during a time of global conflict. After World War II, Captain America was temporarily retired, but he was later revived in the Silver Age of Comic Books in the 1960s.

One of the key elements of Captain America's character is his unwavering commitment to justice, freedom, and the American ideals of democracy. He is known for his strong moral code and sense of duty, often serving as a moral compass for the superhero community. Despite his enhanced physical abilities, Captain America is celebrated not just for his strength, agility, and combat skills, but also for his leadership qualities and strategic mind. In addition to his solo adventures, Captain America is a founding member of the superhero team known as the Avengers. He has played a crucial role in many major Marvel Comics storylines, including the "Civil War" and "Winter Soldier" arcs. The character has been adapted into various forms of media, including animated television shows, video games, and, notably, the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU).

In the MCU, Captain America is portrayed by actor Chris Evans. The character's appearances in films like "Captain America: The First Avenger," "The Winter Soldier," and "Civil War" have contributed to the widespread popularity and recognition of the iconic superhero in contemporary popular culture. Captain America's shield has become an enduring symbol, representing not only the character but also the ideals for which he stands.
